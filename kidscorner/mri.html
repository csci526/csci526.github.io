What is MRI?

MRI stands for Magnetic Resonance Imaging. Using magnets, takes a picture of your brain so we can see what it looks like, and how it functions. This is what your day of MRI will look like!

1. Prepare for your MRI day:

Arrive well rested. Taking a picture of your brain requires you to lie very very still for about 40 minutes (with short wiggle breaks in between). To get a good picture, we want you to be awake the whole time! Make sure you get a good night’s rest so you can be awake for all the fun!

Watch our MRI video! We want you to be as prepared and comfortable as possible!

2. When you arrive, we will take you first to a practice MRI room.

In the practice room, we will go over what your MRI experience will look and sound like. We will practice going in the fake scanner so you are extra comfortable with how it feels!

Then, we will practice the fun game that you will play in the real camera.

Lastly, we will pick a movie for you to watch as your picture is being taken.

3. Next, it’s time to go in the real MRI room!

When you get to the real MRI room, you will meet more of our friendly researchers. We will ask you to remove your shoes, and any metal you have (bracelets, earrings, belts, etc.). Just to make sure you don’t have any metal, we will check with a metal detector wand!

When everything is ready, we’ll show you the camera!

4. Get picture ready...

You now will get to sit on a comfy bed with a blanket and a stuffed toy, if you’d like.

We will give you earplugs and noise-cancelling headphones so you can hear your movie, and us!

Then, we’ll put a special helmet above your head, and raise up the bed like a spaceship. When you’re ready, the bed will slide into the camera, and you’re ready to go!!

5. Once you’re in the MRI...

We can talk to you and you can talk to us!

We’ll start by playing the movie you picked out. While you watch the movie, the camera will take pictures. Every time it takes a picture, it makes a sound (just like a real camera makes a “click”). These sounds mean that your brain is being photographed! Click {here} to hear some of the sounds that the camera makes!

Then, you’ll play a fun game-- and you’re all done!
