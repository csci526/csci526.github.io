What is EEG?

EEG stands for electroencephalography, and is used to measure activity in the brain. A special cap is placed on your head that allows us to see the patterns happening in your brain. This is what your day with EEG looks like!

1. Prepare for your EEG day:

Arrive with clean, dry hair.

Because we pick up your brain activity from your head, having clean hair is very helpful, and makes your EEG experience faster and more fun!

Arrive well rested.

While we work as fast as we can, your EEG day will require you to sit still and focus without falling asleep. Get a good night’s rest!

2. When you arrive:

You’ll be led to our EEG room.

We will turn on a cartoon, so you’ll be entertained.

Then, you’ll sit in a yellow chair, while we gently brush your hair and clean a few spots on your face.

Next, we will measure your head with a soft measuring tape {picture}, and make a small, temporary red mark on your forehead with a washable crayon. (At the end, this will be washed off completely).

Then, we will place a few wires, called electrodes, on your face, just like the picture below. These electrodes are filled with a water-based gel that may be a little cold.

Then, we will place the special cap on your head!

3. Then, the games begin:

After the cap is on and comfortable, we will move you into a special room, where you will sit in a big, comfy chair.

We will give you a button box and show you how to play our games!

4. All done!

When the games are done, we remove the cap, wash off the red mark, and you’re ready to go! Great job!
